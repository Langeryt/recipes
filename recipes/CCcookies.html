Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies are a beloved classic in the world of baking, known for their irresistible combination of buttery, chewy dough and sweet, melty chocolate chips. Originating in the United States, these cookies have become a universal treat enjoyed by people of all ages. The dough is typically made with common ingredients such as flour, sugar, butter, and eggs, while the star of the show—the chocolate chips—adds a decadent touch. The distinctive aroma of freshly baked chocolate chip cookies wafting from the oven is enough to evoke feelings of comfort and nostalgia. Whether enjoyed with a glass of milk or on their own, these delightful cookies are a timeless indulgence that continues to bring joy to cookie enthusiasts around the globe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Gather your ingredients and soften butter
  2. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  3. Wisk butter, white sugar, and brown sugar with an electric mixer in a large bowl until smooth
  4. Beat in eggs then add in vanilla
  5. Add baking soda to batter along with salt
  6. Stir in flour and chocolate chips
  7. Drop spoonfuls of dough on to baking sheets
  8. Bake in the preheated oven until edges are nicely browned
  9. Cool on the baking sheets briefly before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.
